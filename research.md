## Research overview

My work lies at the intersection of machine learning, graph-based modeling, time series analysis, and interpretable AI. I am particularly interested in how contextual and structural dependencies can be modeled to support prediction and decision-making in complex environments.

## Main themes

### Graph representation learning
I develop graph-based learning frameworks for relational and evolving data, with emphasis on representation learning, node classification, and dynamic graph modeling.

### Co-evolving time series
My research investigates how multiple time series influence one another and how these dependencies can be exploited for forecasting, representation learning, and model selection.

### Survival analysis and event prediction
I explore methods for modeling time-to-event data in dynamic settings, especially when events depend on structural, temporal, and contextual factors.

### Context-aware and explainable AI
A central goal of my research is to build models that not only predict well but also provide interpretable descriptions of the context in which events emerge.

## Application domains

- Social network analysis
- Cybersecurity and intrusion detection
- Fraud detection and intelligent auditing
- Health data analysis
- Sports analytics and predictive modeling
